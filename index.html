AMTRAK STATIONS - SUMMARY

Amtrak is celebrating its 50th anniversary this year. Amtrak operates on a wide network covering 46 states of the United States with 500 destinations. It is the only high-speed intercity passenger rail carrier in the United States, capable of reaching speeds of up to 150 mph (241 kph). Amtrak customers make roughly 46,200 journeys every day on average. As per the data(2012) given in the table, the busiest station with highest number of passengers is New York (Penn Station), New York and Philadelphia (30th St), Pennsylvania respectively. Amtrak trains travel on tracks that belong to other railways for 75% of their journey. "Host railways" include big, publicly listed firms based in the United States or Canada, as well as state and local government organizations and small businesses.


The majority of Amtrak stations are located in higher areas. The station located at the highest elevation is Frisco, Colorado with 9070ft. Amtrak continued to upgrade important station facilities on the NEC in FY 2020 for better operations and a better customer experience. In many locations where intercity bus and plane service are unavailable, Amtrak is the only intercity passenger transit option. Amtrak employs almost 21,000 employees from 46 different states, with a total annual payroll of more than $1.6 billion. Fort Worth, Texas is a central station and is famous for its soaring clock tower. The train route between New York, N.Y. (Penn Station) and Montreal (Gare Centrale), Quebec is the most scenic Amtrak route.


According to the data(2012) given in the table, Columbia, South Carolina is the only station that handles US Mail service. There are 20 Amtrak stations that does not observe any Day light savings. 35 Amtrak stations have MetrolinkTVM. There are 15 Amtrak stations with Lounge facilities. There are 4 types of stations namely Curbside Bus Stop only (no shelter), Platform only (no shelter), Platform with Shelter, Station Building (with waiting room). Amongst all stations, 684 stations do not have any staff whereas the rest 220 stations are staffed. The stations that have staff are equipped with ticket office and the rest don’t have a ticket office. Amtrak stations have different time zones. There are 186 stations following Central Time Zone (CST), 339 stations following Eastern Time Zone (EST), 63 stations following Mountain time, and 316 stations following Pacific Standard Time Zone (PST). 119 stations out of 904 stations handle Amtrak Express trains. The Texas Eagle is Amtrak's longest (and oddly presidential) route, running between Chicago, Illinois to Los Angeles, California.


Almost all Amtrak stations are equipped with an enclosed waiting area, Wi-Fi, parking area, accessible platform, and wheelchair facilities. There is no inter-city passenger rail service to the states of South Dakota or Wyoming. Kansas City, Missouri is a popular Amtrak station featuring its Beaux Arts glory, a planetarium and a rail history exhibit with main hall containing 3,500-pound chandeliers hanging overhead. Washington District of Columbia is known for its 22-karat gold leaf decorating the ceiling and 46 statues of Roman soldiers. It is famous for its underground shopping concourse. Because safety is a priority at Amtrak stations, the Amtrak Police keeps a close eye on all activity in the terminals and on the trains. Philadelphia (30th St), Pennsylvania is famous for its five-story wall windows and the glorious chandelier lighting. Union Terminal, Cincinnati is recently transformed into Cincinnati Museum center. People travelling nearby Richmond will witness the Main Street Station, Richmond’s six floor brick clocktower. Interior is a mimic of an original Victorian-age building. In the future Amtrak aims to be the biggest and fastest transportation network in the US which will exceed the expectation of its customers.
